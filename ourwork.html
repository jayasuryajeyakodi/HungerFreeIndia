What we do in Hunger Free India Foundation?

Hunger Free India Foundation is one of the leading NGOs in India. We have helped 15,000+ individuals with our focus on making a difference by providing quality education to financially challenged children. 

Eradicating Poverty

It would be incorrect to say that all poverty reduction programmes have failed. The growth of the middle class (which was virtually non-existent when India became a free nation in August 1947) indicates that economic prosperity has indeed been very impressive in India, but the DISTRIBUTION OF WEALTH has been very uneven.


Eradication of poverty can only be a very long-term goal in India .


To achieve a higher rate of poverty reduction, India will need to address the inequalities in opportunities that impede poor people from participating in the growth process

Little Flower

Little Flower is a home for parentless and neglected girls. Located in Manapakkam (within the Chennai metropolitan area), this Home has a ventilated dormitory with bunker beds, attached bathrooms, a study hall, a library, a computer room and a playground. The girls of our homes are provided the necessary provisions with extreme care and attention. 

The children are nurtured physically, mentally and emotionally through morning prayer, evening play, adequate and timely food, clothing and special attention to their studies. We provide all the essentials for each child’s balanced growth as well as to cultivate an optimistic mind-set. We facilitate them to obtain a strong educational foundation and train them in a suitable vocation for self-support.

MGR Deaf and Dumb School

Dr.MGR Home & Higher Secondary School for the Speech and Hearing Impaired was established on 17th January 1990, as per the wishes of Dr.M.G.Ramachandran who was considered to be a great humanitarian and Chief Minister of the State of Tamil Nadu in India 


We helped the students with education and periodically provide them meals to eradicate hunger.

Helping the Elderly

Many elderly people are abandoned by their family and left alone in homes. We help them lead a life in peace by volunteering time with them and providing them health meals

​​Back to School

Back to School is our annual initiative as part of which we give ‘School Kit’ to rural kids.

The School kit consists of school bag, notebooks, uniforms, shoes and all other items as per the child needs for attending school.
